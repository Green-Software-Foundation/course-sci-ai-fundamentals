## The Exclusions — A Thought Experiment

Now I want to talk about what the SCI deliberately excludes. This is one of the most important design decisions we made.

Let me give you a thought experiment.

Imagine two teams building software products.

**Team A** does everything right. They make their code energy efficient. They implement carbon-aware scheduling to run workloads when the grid is cleaner. They optimise their hardware utilisation. All of this takes significant time and engineering investment.

**Team B** doesn't do any of that. Their code is inefficient. They don't think about when or where they run workloads. Instead, they just purchase Renewable Energy Credits and carbon offsets.

Now, you could argue the net impact is similar. Both teams end up with something that looks green on paper.

But here's the thing: **the SCI is not about calculating net impact. It's about incentivising action.**

We did not want "buy RECs" or "buy offsets" to be actions that reduce your SCI score. We wanted the score to go down only when you:

- Reduce energy consumption
- Use cleaner energy at the source
- Use less hardware

So the SCI deliberately excludes market-based measures:

- Carbon offsets and credits
- Renewable Energy Credits
- Power Purchase Agreements

**[VISUAL: Team A vs Team B. Team A's SCI goes down. Team B's stays the same.]**

Now, I want to be very clear about something. This is not an anti-offset position.

Many of the people who actively developed the SCI also actively advocate for purchasing RECs, PPAs, and offsets. They're an essential part of any comprehensive climate strategy.

But we treat them as two separate things.

Offsets and RECs are part of your climate strategy. You should absolutely invest in them. Report them in your sustainability disclosures.

The SCI is a different tool with a different purpose. It's a metric that goes down only when you do energy efficiency, hardware efficiency, or carbon awareness.

Offsets are essential. But offsets are not eliminations. The SCI only rewards elimination.
