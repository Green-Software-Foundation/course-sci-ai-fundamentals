## Part C: Intentional Design

So that's the theory. Now let me tell you how the SCI actually came to be.

Many of the people who helped create the SCI had been trying to measure software emissions using the GHG Protocol for Corporate Reporting. And they kept running into the same problem: the metric was insensitive to the changes they were making.

You could optimise your code, make it significantly more energy efficient, and your GHG total as calculated by your sustainability department wouldn't budge. Or worse, it would go up because your user base grew.

That was incredibly frustrating. People were doing the right thing and the metric wasn't rewarding it.

So when the Green Software Foundation set out to create a new specification, we didn't start by asking "how do we calculate how much carbon software emits?" We started with a different question: "what actions do we want to incentivise?"

We identified three specific actions:

**Energy efficiency** — using less electricity to perform the same work.

**Hardware efficiency** — using less physical hardware to perform the same work.

**Carbon awareness** — shifting when or where you run software to take advantage of cleaner electricity.

And then we designed a metric that would reward all three. Not by accident. By design.

We wanted to have confidence. We wanted to guarantee that if you make your software more energy efficient, your score goes down. If you make it more hardware efficient, your score goes down. If you make it more carbon aware, your score goes down.

**[VISUAL: Three icons — lightbulb, server, globe with sun. Arrow showing "Do these things → SCI goes down"]**

That's the intentionality behind the SCI. It wasn't designed to be a comprehensive carbon accounting tool. It was designed to be a metric that drives the right actions.

And if you think about it in terms of those two boundaries: we made sure the inclusion boundary of the SCI captures energy, hardware, and carbon intensity. And those are things software teams have agency over. Where those boundaries overlap is exactly where energy efficiency, hardware efficiency, and carbon awareness live.

That overlap is where the SCI lives. That's why it drives action.
